Theorem

Prove that √2 is irrational.

Proof

Suppose, for contradiction, that √2 is rational. Then √2 = p / q where p, q ∈ ℤ and gcd(p, q) = 1. Squaring both sides gives p2 = 2q2, so p2 is even, hence p is even. Writing p = 2k yields q2 = 2k2, meaning q is also even — contradicting gcd(p, q) = 1. Therefore √2 is irrational. ■
